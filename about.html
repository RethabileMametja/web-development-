How It All Began

A Vision Takes Root

A Vision Takes Root

In 2019, a small group of environmentally conscious neighbors in Melbourne's inner suburbs noticed something troubling. Despite living in one of the world's most livable cities, their community lacked green spaces where people could connect with nature and each other.

Sarah Chen, a local teacher and sustainability advocate, organized the first community meeting in her backyard. What started as a conversation between five neighbors about growing their own food quickly blossomed into something much bigger.

"We realized we all shared the same dream," recalls Sarah. "A place where children could learn where food comes from, where neighbors could share knowledge and resources, and where we could all contribute to a more sustainable future."

Our Journey Through the Years

Timeline of Key Milestones

2019

The Foundation

First community meeting held. Local council approves our proposal for the unused lot on Green Street. 15 founding members sign up.

2020

Breaking Ground

Despite pandemic challenges, we cleared the land and built our first 20 garden plots. Community composting system established.

2021

Growing Community

Membership grows to 75 people. First annual harvest festival held. Educational workshop program launches with monthly sessions.

2022

Expanding Horizons

Youth program begins with local schools. Greenhouse construction completed. Native plant section added to promote biodiversity.

2023

Recognition & Growth

Awarded "Best Community Initiative" by Melbourne City Council. Membership reaches 150. Solar-powered irrigation system installed.

2024

Sustainable Future

Expansion to 80 plots completed. Partnership with local restaurants for fresh produce supply. 200+ active members achieved.
